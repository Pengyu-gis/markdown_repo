# GIStory Issue 13 | Cui Yunhe: A Dynamic Life Journey of Maintaining and Adjusting Mindset

## Guest Introduction

Cui Yunhe received her bachelor's degree from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC) (double major in Geographic Information Science and Cartography & Geology), her master's degree in Applied Urban Informatics from New York University (NYU), and her PhD in Geography from the University of Connecticut (UConn). From 2022 to 2023, she worked as a Research Assistant at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL). Since 2023, she has been working as a Data Engineer at JPMorgan Chase & Co.

## 01 The Winding Path of Exploring Academic Directions

Cui Yunhe's academic journey began with a major in **Geology** at China University of Geosciences (Beijing). After completing two years of domestic study, she developed a strong interest in overseas educational resources. She was particularly drawn to the **rich research opportunities for undergraduates** offered by UIUC. In 2015, Cui decided to transfer to UIUC to continue her geology studies. As her understanding of the college and major deepened, she realized she wanted to acquire more geography-related knowledge and techniques, so she began exploring geographic information science and eventually successfully obtained a **double degree in Geology and Geographic Information Science** from UIUC.

When applying for master's programs, Cui Yunhe deliberated for a long time between geology and geographic information science (GIS), ultimately deciding to apply simultaneously for geology, GIS, and data analytics programs. Among the many offers she received, considering the **close ties between NYU's Center for Urban Science and Progress (CUSP) and industry**, and weighing future employment prospects, she ultimately chose NYU.

During her brief one-year master's program from 2018 to 2019, she did not immediately decide whether to enter industry or continue exploring academia after graduation. So she simultaneously prepared for both job applications and PhD applications. This also meant she had to selectively forgo some courses in pursuit of more efficient preparation.

**Hard work pays off.** During this intensive period of study and career preparation, Cui Yunhe finally found a job in California by the end of her second master's semester, while also receiving an offer to pursue PhD studies at UConn. Due to her higher expectations for the job position and her own development, she ultimately chose to continue her academic path at the University of Connecticut (UConn), pursuing a PhD in **transportation geography**.

## 02 Seizing Every Opportunity to Expand Career Possibilities

### The Path to Career Development

In her studies of geology and geographic information science, Cui Yunhe actively learned various new skills. She cherished every opportunity and did her utmost to absorb knowledge and hone her skills. She understood that to master more practical professional skills, one needed to clarify one's career direction and proactively seek relevant learning resources. For example, during her time at NYU, she paid special attention to courses that could provide GIS skills, data visualization, and data analysis and modeling. In class, Cui joined study groups composed of students from different professional backgrounds, and through these groups, engaged in in-depth exchanges with classmates focused on similar career paths. After class, she supplemented her knowledge gaps through online learning and improved her interview performance through repeated mock interviews. Cui Yunhe shared a vivid example to illustrate this point:


&gt; *"If a position has 100 applicants, perhaps only 20 meet all the job requirements. Therefore, the purpose of all learning and interview preparation is to ensure that you become one of these 20 qualified candidates. However, to stand out from these 20 people and ultimately win the position, you also need an extra bit of luck."*

Her previous continuous efforts allowed Cui Yunhe to stand out from interviews when she first tried to find an internship in the U.S. At the end of 2018, among some internship opportunities co-hosted by NYU and local government agencies, she successfully obtained a GIS data analysis-related internship offer at the **New York City Office of the Mayor**. Later, nearing her master's graduation, she found a full-time job in San Diego, California, responsible for processing fire-related data and creating maps. Although job opportunities came one after another, due to the content or timing of these positions not being suitable, she ultimately did not accept these offers and instead directly entered UConn to begin her PhD studies.

In the summer of 2022, she began searching for internship opportunities during her PhD. The skills and knowledge she had accumulated during her PhD in **project management, data analysis and modeling, and data visualization** also helped her secure a research position internship at the **National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL)**. The main content of this internship involved liaising with two state Departments of Transportation (DOTs), deploying NREL-developed accessibility algorithms in cloud environments, and refining related algorithms.

At the same time, in her daily work, she began accumulating understanding of corporate work patterns and cognition and experience of different positions. She gradually realized that she was not very adapted to NREL's work model, so she decided to seek new full-time positions outside of NREL. After nearly **8 months of job searching**, in April 2023, she obtained a full-time **Data Engineer** position at **JPMorgan Chase & Co. (JPMC)**. This position first required candidates to have skills in using Python and SQL to process data, while also requiring candidates to use data orchestration tools, build and maintain data platforms and data products, and process spatial-related data and create web maps for different business groups. Of course, in her work, besides the need to improve data processing and GIS skills, Cui Yunhe also gradually learned some practical experience under business models, such as recognizing the importance of enhancing **business acumen**.

### Workplace Interview Skills Sharing

Cui Yunhe's successful application to JPMC was partly due to her constant preparation for future work positions, and partly due to her emphasis on **building interpersonal relationships**. At that time, while searching for positions, Cui Yunhe happened to see that a JPMC manager was looking for a suitable candidate for a senior position requiring 5 to 7 years of work experience. Although Cui knew that her professional experience at the time did not fully meet the requirements, she was not discouraged and proactively contacted him through **LinkedIn**, introducing her resume and personal strengths. Through this proactive contact, the manager was impressed by her initiative and qualifications and recommended her for an interview. During the interview, the manager further recognized Cui Yunhe's potential and abilities, and decided to hire her after the second technical interview. This experience deepened Cui Yunhe's understanding of the importance of proactive communication and showed her the possibilities it could open in the workplace.

After experiencing rich internships and work, Cui Yunhe shared that the key to interview success lies in **two core factors**:

**First, tailor to fit—pay attention to the match between the individual and the position.** Before an interview, we should customize our resume for the position, allowing our resume to more precisely hit the requirements of the interview position. Through mock interviews, during the interview process, we must be able to accurately and completely demonstrate our abilities. She explained: *"Even if we prepare 100%, if we can only express 60%, we are at a disadvantage in the job competition for that position, because interviewers can hardly dig out from the resume alone the shining points that distinguish us from other candidates."*

**Second, become an interesting candidate.** She suggested adding a section related to personal interests in the resume. She added that such information, although possibly unrelated to the position, can pique the interest of the hiring interviewer and help open a window for conversation and the interview atmosphere. This is also what she considers a humanized approach to interviews:

&gt; *"We should present ourselves as living human beings, and also view the interviewer as a person, not a machine. Such interaction better showcases our personality and authenticity, making us look like a better future colleague to work with, thereby increasing the likelihood of interview success. This method not only helps build better communication but also makes the interview process more natural and relaxed."*


## 03 Valuable Internship Experience Sharing

### Goal-Oriented Early Preparation

Cui Yunhe has always placed great importance on **goal-oriented early preparation**. She believes that although at past time points, every step we took was based on the information and cognition available at that time, such efforts were not necessarily the most effective. Therefore, as personal cognition changes, **continuously adjusting goals and directions of effort** is extremely important for personal development. For example, during her undergraduate studies, she began participating in various company public projects and actively seeking on-campus internships and research projects, and these experiences also laid a solid foundation for her subsequent academic and career paths. By her upper undergraduate years, she strengthened her coursework in mathematical statistics and programming directions and chose to pursue a double degree, making her background more aligned with the needs of recruiting companies. During her master's stage, Cui chose multiple courses related to data processing and modeling to prepare for future data-related work. During her PhD studies, she also continuously participated in online learning programs and took relevant school courses to broaden her professional horizons and skills, continuously enhancing competitiveness and adjusting strategy. Additionally, she recommended that those new to the workplace, besides paying attention to the requirements of currently open positions, also communicate with more experienced colleagues and seniors, because they have already walked the path we are about to take. Learning from them can help us prepare more efficiently. She emphasized that we should use **goal-oriented reflection and evaluation** of our learning behaviors to ensure our efforts point toward the goals we expect. This forward-looking and goal-oriented mindset is one of the important factors in her job search success.

### Later Adjustments Focused on Inner Self

Cui Yunhe deeply understands that even when we have done sufficient early preparation, while striving toward our desired direction, we still need to continuously make necessary adjustments. She believes that in this personalized adjustment process, **listening to one's inner feelings** is extremely critical. For example, during the fall semester of 2022, Cui Yunhe realized that she was more inclined toward an industry work atmosphere, which led her to begin adjusting her career development path. Therefore, Cui recommends that students also closely monitor their feelings, reflect on their current direction of effort, and make adjustments based on their inner selves, choosing a career path more suitable for themselves. She believes that only in this way can we face and enjoy every challenge encountered in the process of moving forward over the long term. She also shared some of her personal experiences to encourage students to better understand the relationship between self-feelings and career pressure:

&gt; *"Life itself is a challenge. We don't need to be too harsh on ourselves, nor feel guilty because our results are not perfect. Learning to accept ourselves and make peace with ourselves is a very important lesson when facing pressure and challenges."*

### Looking Back at the Journey of Sparkling Stars

After experiencing work in different positions, Cui Yunhe emphasizes that when facing internship choices during the PhD, **salary is not the most important consideration factor**. The **reputation of the company and position and the development opportunities they bring** should be the deciding factors. She believes that the short-term attraction of salary is far less valuable than the long-term value brought by a high-quality internship position. Additionally, she mentioned the need to balance on-campus and off-campus activities in terms of time arrangement, to ensure that on-campus research is not affected while fully utilizing off-campus internship opportunities to explore future directions. Cui Yunhe places great importance on the feelings during the process, believing that only by personally experiencing a specific work environment and content can one accurately judge whether they are suited to that environment. For example, after experiencing the internship at NREL, Cui realized that NREL's work had its own characteristics, and her own traits were not fully aligned with the needs of this job. This also paved the way for her later path: despite the more severe employment environment, the challenges of re-searching for a full-time job were greater than directly accepting a full-time offer from the internship unit, but she still chose to continue job hunting in industry.

Nearly two years of full-time work and internships gave Cui Yunhe a deeper understanding of the work styles in academia and industry. She believes that in academia, an individual's **hard power** (such as the quality and quantity of published articles) is a more important measure; while in industry, an individual's **expression and communication abilities** are more important. She explained that when working in industry, we need to frequently communicate with internal or external clients from different backgrounds, and when facing clients' diverse backgrounds, we cannot assume they understand our field and comprehend our professional terminology. For example, for those of us in the geographic information science field, visualizing tabular data and displaying it on a map is technically simple, and the results are quite attractive to laypeople, but clients may not fully understand the technology we use and the support we need. Therefore, **accurately and clearly conveying and "translating" business needs** is crucial in actual work advancement.

In the above job search process, Cui Yunhe believes there is a consistent key point: **building networks (networking)**. Cui believes that working in an industry and getting to know more practitioners can provide highly timely industry and position information. There are also many ways to build interpersonal relationships: we can actively participate in industry conferences to meet more practitioners; during internships, we can proactively get to know and communicate with colleagues; on online platforms such as LinkedIn, we can actively invite relevant professionals for online or in-person coffee chats; on campus, we can communicate more with teachers, classmates, and seniors.

### Looking Ahead to Future Paths

Although we need to continuously adjust when facing a dynamic life process, Cui Yunhe does not recommend **frequent job-hopping in the early stages of one's career**, as this may affect future employers' views of interview candidates, including perceptions of loyalty to the company, personal career planning ability, and work stability. Beyond this, Cui Yunhe also places great emphasis on communication with superiors and colleagues at work. At work, she regularly discusses with colleagues about their work experiences and the expectations that colleagues in management positions have for their subordinates. Through this approach, she can not only understand internal position needs but also gain insight into what backgrounds, skills, and traits make new employees more attractive in the workplace. At the same time, this dialogue can provide a great deal of information beyond daily responsibilities, helping her timely supplement knowledge and skills to adapt to more comprehensive or different job positions. Cui Yunhe emphasizes that this ability to acquire information is even more important in the current context of frequent corporate layoffs. If one can, while building a **"personal brand,"** find a potential backup position for oneself within the current company, establishing connections and leaving a positive impression in daily work, the challenges regarding work identity for international students are smaller compared to jumping to another company. She recommends that we should always maintain sensitivity to recruitment market dynamics, continuously learn, and maintain our competitiveness in the market, ensuring that we can develop steadily even in turbulent market environments.

## 04 Life and Work Are Equally Important

While pursuing her PhD, Cui Yunhe developed her own ways to relieve stress. First, she particularly cherished time spent with friends and family. In daily life, chatting more with friends can relieve academic pressure. Second, she found that participating in some extracurricular activities, such as **sports and shopping**, could also effectively relieve stress. Cui Yunhe believes that human willpower is limited, and timely stress regulation is very necessary. One should not be too harsh with oneself, and should not be stingy with self-praise. She firmly believes that people are not machines—we all need appropriate rest space. Although reviewing one's own behavior is a good habit, she reminds us that we do not need to over-review ourselves. Time does not flow backward, and no one can be perfect. Using standards from repeated rehearsals to evaluate oneself at that time is unfair. Through such methods, Cui Yunhe found ways to maintain balance between heavy study, work, and personal life, which also helped her improve her overall life satisfaction.

## 05 Message

In the process of pursuing career goals and应对 life challenges, we often encounter various uncertainties and pressures. In these moments, communicating, sharing, and confiding with friends can often bring us new inspiration and necessary support. Because life's journey is not always smooth sailing, setbacks and difficulties are inevitable along the way. So when facing challenges, we should not be too harsh on ourselves. We should learn to accept our own shortcomings while also valuing our strengths and achievements. It is precisely these experiences that shape a stronger and more mature self. Therefore, no matter what environment we are in or what challenges we face, maintaining an **optimistic and resilient mindset** is crucial. Believing in our own abilities and potential, and bravely stepping forward, is an important driving force for us to overcome difficulties and achieve our goals.
